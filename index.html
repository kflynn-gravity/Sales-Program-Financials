Performance & The SAE Scorecard

Sales Above Expected (SAE): Our scorecard is a clear, mission-aligned way to measure and manage performance.

About the SAE Score

The SAE Score is a metric, similar to baseball's WAR, that measures a sales representative's performance relative to the average. A score of **0** represents an average performer, a negative score indicates underperformance, and a positive score indicates overperformance.

  • üõëNegative Score: Immediate exit. This rep is performing below the break-even point.
  • ‚ö†Ô∏èScore of zero or greater than zero: Targeted coaching and support. This rep is meeting the minimum performance required to be profitable.
  • ‚úÖScore greater than a pre-defined profitable benchmark: Eligibility for the next phase of growth. This rep is a top performer.

Compensation, Costs & ROI

Compensation Model: Associates will be paid the greater of $20/hr (for an 8-hour day) or $20 per qualified appointment set.

Cost Projection: For a pilot of 6 associates, the total monthly cost is approximately $20,784.

The Compounding ROI: The true value is in the long-term revenue stream. For every 1 associate, we project:

30-Day Fail Case: 2 New Accounts Per Month

  • Monthly Cost (Per Rep): ~$3,464
  • New Accounts (30 Days): 2
  • Monthly Revenue: `2 accounts x $447` = **$894**
  • Net Loss (30 Days): `(Monthly Cost) - (Monthly Revenue)` = `$3,464 - $894` = **-$2,570**

This scenario demonstrates a clear negative SAE score, triggering an immediate off-ramp and minimizing losses.

Why a 6-Rep Cohort is More Efficient

Our analysis shows that a cohort of 6 associates is the most efficient starting point. The primary benefits of this focused cohort are:

  • Focus and Specialization: By dedicating a team to a single task‚Äîsetting appointments‚Äîwe maximize their efficiency and build a predictable engine for future sales.
  • Data-Driven Management: The size of the cohort provides a sufficient sample size to satisfactorily evaluate the program's effectiveness, allowing for informed decisions on a "fail-fast" basis.
  • Cost-Effectiveness: A 6-rep team is large enough to absorb initial fixed costs without incurring the diminishing returns of a larger, less agile team.

Key Risks & Mitigation

Risk Mitigation Strategy
Brand Misrepresentation A simple script, focused 3-day onboarding, and a Quality Score in our SAE metric to monitor and coach on professionalism.
Poor Lead Hand-off Associates will be trained to take detailed notes and a clear process will ensure full-time reps review these notes to create a seamless transition.
Legal Issues The hourly W-2 model and early buy-in from Legal and HR mitigate misclassification risk.
Market Saturation We will track saturation and have a plan to rotate associates or markets as needed if we scale.
Internal Pushback This program is positioned to support and increase the success of full-time reps, not to replace them.
